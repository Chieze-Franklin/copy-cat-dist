Detect duplicate messages in Slack channels.

Slack is a wonderful place to share ideas, resources or even jokes. But, sometimes, we are all too eager to do so without first checking if the item we are about to share have already been shared in that same channel recently. This leads to polluted channels with duplicate messages, and negatively impacts the experiences of all members of the channel.

Install CopyCat in your Slack workspace, and invite it to the relevant channels so that you (and other members) can be informed when the message just shared has already been shared recently. Not only does CopyCat flag duplicate messages, it attaches links to the original messages.

Amazing Features

Easy to Use

After installing the CopyCat app in your Slack workspace ( ) all you need to invite it into the channels in which you want it to be operational.
/invite @copycat

All Message Types

CopyCat detects duplicates not only amongst text messages but messages containing emojis, messages containing urls, and messages containing uploaded files of all types.

Privacy

CopyCat does not store or share the messages in your channels with any party. Messages are only fetched when CopyCat needs to compare a new message with existing messages.

24/7 availability

CopyCat is always available, and always working to detect duplicates.

Updates?

Regular Updates!

From time to time the app will be updated to enhance its capabilities. Currently it only considers exact matches when determining duplicates but the plan is to make it more flexible. For instance, it may be able to find messages that are not exact but close enough. It may also be able to understand the context and intents in messages, and use those to point out other similar messages. This will be very helpful in channels that are mostly about enquiries. That app may be able to look at a just-posted question, and figure out a past post that is similar (in context and intent) to the just-posted question, and point the user to that past post (because the answer the user needs may be one of the replies of the past post).
